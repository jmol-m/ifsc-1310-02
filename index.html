Web Accesibility Writing Assignment

In my personal opinion, Web Accessibility is one of the most important facets of Web Design. While many may argue the tried and true premise that better accessibility equates to expanded user outreach, I argue that better accessibility may even bolster the loyalty of existing users for which certain accessibility features may not have been designed for. An example of this is icon presentation, which was spoken about by Derek Featherstone. Icons have become a staple on the web, but their initial intended function was to aid the visually impaired and colorblind; despite this, high contrast images which facilitate information on the web are enjoyed by able-bodied users and users that require accessibility features alike.

Good web accessibility has always been met with positive results, but I’d argue that in this day and age they are even more important than ever. When many people think of those who may need to use accessibility features, they may think of the blind, partially sighted, deaf, hard of hearing, or individuals with reduced mobility; what many don't know, however, is that ailments such as Myopia are becoming increasingly common, and accessibility features benefit the near-sighted just as much as the partially sighted. By 2050, Myopia is projected to exist in almost 50 percent of the world’s population, and in Eastern Asian regions, these statistics are even more alarming. While glasses do exist, individuals with high Myopia who take their phones to bed – like myself – often don't wear their glasses to bed, so better visual accessibility and understandability is no longer an option, but instead an expectation.

Thoughts on Progressive Enhancement

Progressive enhancement is essentially the practice of prioritizing that the content of a website works throughout the usable majority of browsers and all their variations. In order, the focus is on the content-rich XHTML markup which conveys the purpose of the site. Then prioritize making it more presentable with CSS styling, then finally add interactivity through Javascript. As opposed to the methodology of graceful degradation, which treats structuring the content of a site for older versions of browsers as an afterthought, progressive enhancement puts the practice of careful structuring of content at the forefront, which allows it to naturally degrade far more gracefully.
